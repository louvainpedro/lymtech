Transform your Black Friday sales with effective integration

As the much-awaited Black Friday approaches, we at Lymtech are committed to sharing innovative strategies to boost sales for our partners and customers. This year, we have made every effort to provide integration solutions, especially for e-commerce companies operating in the market. Making the connection between data in your systems as fluid and efficient as it is with us.

Maximizing the customer experience

Customer experience is a fundamental part of any business and the strategy we propose stands out as the key to satisfying the growing expectations of 21st century consumers. In the article “Integration with Marketplaces: A Fundamental Piece for Success”, we explain how solutions like ours are beneficial for any organization that sells products online and can transform the purchasing journey, delivering integration and personalization for our customers.

Data reveals that approximately 70% of customers expect a conversational service when interacting with a company. Surprisingly, today less than half of companies offer quality conversational experiences. With the help of our consultancy, we can both improve the fluidity of this communication and leave all the necessary conversations in one place to best serve your client.

Digital Reliability

We also highlight the importance of trust in the digital environment, especially at a time when consumers are more concerned about the security and confidentiality of their personal information. According to research by Conversion, the purchasing intention of Brazilians on this date should increase by 9% compared to 2022. The trend towards e-commerce is great, with 72% of the public admitting to preferring this type of purchase. And this number becomes even more significant when we take into account that the purchase intention during BLACK FRIDAY this year is 90.5%.

The future of retail

In the WebPan article “Even with the reopening of physical stores, e-commerce will grow on Black Friday”, we can see how the pandemic accelerated digital transformation and online purchasing behavior. Criteo's research has since demonstrated that the integration between physical and online sales would not just be a trend, but a reality for the retail sector.

The “continuous commerce” mentality is increasingly important, where consumers are always thinking about their next purchase. This represents a significant opportunity for companies to adopt a connected approach, demonstrated by consumers' preference for retailers that offer both options.
